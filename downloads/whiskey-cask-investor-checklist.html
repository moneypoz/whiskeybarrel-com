12 Questions to Ask Before You Wire a Dollar
Section 1

Ownership Documentation

1. Will the cask be registered in my name at the warehouse level with a delivery order, not just a platform certificate?
A platform certificate is an IOU. A warehouse delivery order is title to a physical asset you can claim independently.
2. Can I contact the warehouse directly and independently verify my cask exists and is registered to me?
If verification only runs through the platform, you have no way to confirm the cask is real without their cooperation.
3. If the platform ceased trading tomorrow, what documentation do I have to prove ownership and claim my cask?
Your recovery in an insolvency depends entirely on the paperwork you hold today, not on promises made at the point of sale.

Section 2

Fees and Costs

4. What is the total exit commission, including any administrative or transfer fees on top of the headline percentage?
The advertised commission is rarely the whole story. Stacked admin and transfer fees quietly erode your realized return.
5. Is storage and insurance included in the purchase price, or billed separately? For how many years?
"Included" often means included for a fixed term. Know when the meter starts running and how much it costs after that.
6. What is my realistic total cost of ownership over the full holding period, including angel's share volume loss?
Evaporation shrinks the asset itself every year. A complete cost picture nets fees, storage, insurance, and lost volume.

Section 3

Exit and Liquidity

7. What are my exit options beyond the platform's own buy-back program?
If the only buyer is the platform that sold to you, you are price-taking from a single counterparty with no competition.
8. Can the platform name specific bottlers, auction houses, or secondary buyers they have worked with in the last 12 months?
Named, recent, verifiable buyers prove a real exit market exists. Vague claims of "strong demand" do not.
9. What happens to my exit options if the platform's buy-back program is suspended or the company goes into administration?
Buy-back programs are discretionary and can be paused. Know your independent route to sale before you need it.

Section 4

Regulation and Recourse

10. Is this platform operating under US securities law, or is it a foreign unregulated product?
Regulatory status determines what protections you have. An unregulated offshore product may offer almost none.
11. If something goes wrong, which regulator can I contact and which courts have jurisdiction?
A concrete regulator and forum is your recourse. If no one can name them, you may have none.
12. Is this investment IRA eligible, and if so, which custodians does the platform work with?
IRA eligibility requires a willing self-directed custodian. Named custodians signal the structure has been done before, correctly.
